What happens when an artist’s urgent message for the times is seen as filthy, obscene, or socially corrupt? Concert Cancel Culture examines the phenomenon of protest movements that call for the cancellation of international concerts on religious or social grounds. Drawing on the author’s firsthand experiences of religious life in Korea and Indonesia, it explores the contradictions of faith and the cultural limits imposed by excessive religious fervor.
